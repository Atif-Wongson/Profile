About

Hello I'm Atif Wongson

I’m someone who finds joy in the quiet, hands-on process of making food that takes time — like cheese, bread, fermented things, and charcuterie. I’m drawn to traditions, to microbes, to fermentation, to transformation — the kind of food that you don’t just “make,” but actually live with day by day.

I love using my hands. I love watching something raw and simple become alive with time, whether it’s milk turning into cheese, flour becoming a loaf, or meat slowly curing into something deep and flavorful. There’s patience in that. Humility, too. And it gives me a kind of grounding that nothing else does.

While I’ve worked in many kitchen roles, what I always come back to is this sense of being a craftsman — not chasing speed or perfection, but being present with the process. That’s where I feel most like myself.
